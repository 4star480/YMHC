Who we are

Inspired by leading African mental-health movements, YMHC is a youth-focused collaborative building safer conversations, stronger systems, and scalable community solutions.

Our vision

An Africa where every young person can access compassionate mental health support without shame — in schools, clinics, and communities.

Our mission

To generate local evidence, train frontline workers, and deliver outreach that reduces stigma and connects youth to care.

Our values

Youth leadership, cultural humility, data-driven practice, and partnerships that last beyond a single project cycle.

What we do

Three interconnected programs — the heart of everything YMHC delivers.

🔬

Research

We design and support studies on youth mental health in African contexts — prevalence, help-seeking, digital wellbeing, and program effectiveness.

  • Community-based surveys & focus groups
  • Partnerships with universities & hospitals
  • Open summaries for policymakers
  • Ethics-first, youth-inclusive methods
📚

Capacity building

We equip teachers, peer supporters, CHWs, and NGO staff with practical skills to recognize distress and refer safely.

  • MHFA-style workshops & toolkits
  • Supervision for lay counselors
  • Corporate & school wellness training
  • Online webinars & certificate tracks
🤝

Community outreaches

We meet young people where they are — campuses, markets, faith spaces, and online — with talks, screenings, and safe-space dialogues.

  • School mental health weeks
  • Anti-stigma campaigns
  • Group support & listening circles
  • Linkage to clinical care when needed
